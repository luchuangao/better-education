# Give Children Time

![Give Children Time](https://coresg-normal.trae.ai/api/ide/v1/text_to_image?prompt=Minimalist%20book%20illustration%2C%20warm%20colors%2C%20a%20child%20focused%20on%20building%20blocks%20or%20reading%20on%20the%20floor%2C%20no%20TV%20or%20phones%20around%2C%20peaceful%20and%20quiet%20atmosphere%2C%20soft%20lighting%2C%20high%20quality&image_size=landscape_16_9)

As an unqualified father who grew up accompanied by television and lacked a concept of time management, I deeply realize the importance of giving children autonomous time. During my growth, television occupied most of my spare time, which only changed when I lived in school during junior high. Therefore, after becoming a father, the first thing I did was not to install a TV at home, and we still don't have one, allowing the child more time to play outdoors, read, and play with toys. This has cultivated his concentration well since childhood.

Nowadays, mobile phones have gradually replaced television as the main tool for consuming time. We did not completely ban the child from using mobile phones; some learning tasks need to be completed through mobile phones, and he also has the freedom to watch cartoons and play games. But through family communication, we reached a consensus: no more than 20 minutes per use, and no more than 1 hour per day in total. This rule is not an imposed regulation, but a principle agreed upon by the whole family. Only by controlling screen time can he have his own free time to explore things he is truly interested in.

From the kindergarten stage, we made a daily list with him to learn how to allocate time reasonably, completing necessary tasks while reserving a large amount of free time. Sometimes when he plays outside and forgets to go home for dinner, we don't force him, just remind him. If he chooses to play for a while longer, then he needs to eat by himself and wash the dishes after returning home, which is also agreed upon in advance.

> Summerhill School's educational philosophy: "Make the school fit the child - instead of making the child fit the school."

Such an educational environment may be rare in China, but I always believe that the best education starts at the family. We should strive to "**make the family fit the child, instead of making the child fit the family.**" Giving children the right to choose and time they can arrange independently is helping them build their own sense of time and rhythm of life.
