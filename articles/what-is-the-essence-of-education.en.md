# What Is the Essence of Education

The essence of education is awakening and empowerment, letting children recognize their potential and helping them realize their ideals. The core characteristic of exam-oriented education lies in the pursuit of certainty. This desire for certainty largely stems from our lack of religious beliefs, lack of pursuit beyond material things, and lack of reverence for natural laws. But it is not completely without faith; people often believe in money and power, view everything with a utilitarian heart, and judge heroes by success or failure.

So is it necessarily better to have religious beliefs? Not necessarily. People with true pursuits can transcend the limitations of religion to explore science and truth. Where does our cohesion come from? It mainly comes from power. Whoever holds power has the right to define systems and standards, and thus dominate behavioral norms. However, what those in power say may not be correct, and blind obedience is the poison of the bottom of society.

Every child is born with plasticity. I think the best education is family education, because it is the parents who have the deepest influence on children, not teachers, homework or textbooks. Children should be allowed to play more, try more, and contact nature and new technology from an early age. Through reading and traveling, extensively understand different cultures and countries. Our child reads books about cultures of various countries and has been to two countries so far. You say he is still young and understands nothing, but I think he will imperceptibly understand that other people's ways of doing things and thinking are different from ours, and this difference is the interesting part. Because a closed cultural system is difficult to breed new civilization and creation.
