# Allow Criticism

A prominent feature of education is the pursuit of certainty, and this excessive pursuit often brings many drawbacks. To avoid these drawbacks, the key is to learn to accept criticism, especially as parents, we must accept children's criticism and also be brave in self-criticism.

However, criticism is often ignored in our culture. The most obvious example is that many parents are accustomed to using "obedient" and "good" to measure the quality of children. Criticism is actually the only way to independent thinking; not allowing criticism means that we may have been walking on the wrong path without realizing it, and even use the little power in our hands to make things difficult for children for no reason.

If we specifically dismantle the problems brought about by the pursuit of certainty in education, they mainly include the following aspects:
*   **Excessive exam-oriented**
*   **Emphasis on obedience, suppressing independent thinking**
*   **High cost of failure, small room for error**
*   **Insufficient creativity training**
*   **Lack of emotional and psychological education**
*   **Excessive homogenization, obliterating personality**

In such an environment, "obedient" children are recognized, while "disobedient" children have to bear huge costs. They often need to spend a lifetime digesting this rejection and are prone to falling into a victim mentality, because disobeying the mainstream is like being abandoned by the whole society, rarely recognized, and walking a lonely and difficult hero's road.

For myself, I admit that I have many faults, and I don't mind my child pointing out my problems. Faults are facts and need not be hidden. What we can do is to actively admit and correct, rather than cover up or quibble.

Since it is difficult for us to change the overall standards of education, what we can do is to strengthen ourselves: first, do not use the traditional authoritative education method to treat children at home; second, try our best to strive for a looser growth environment for children, including accumulating certain funds, so that if the child needs it in the future, we can also support him to go abroad to broaden his horizons.

By the way: true patriotism is not shouting and killing all day without seeing the world, but people who still choose to return to build their homeland after going out to see it. They will not turn a blind eye to the country's problems, but dare to criticize and be willing to promote improvement. If the country's problems are not allowed to be criticized, even those who return will choose to leave, because adults who have experienced wind and frost only filter and do not educate.
