# The Importance of Companionship

You can count how much time you can truly spend with your child in a day.

If you have almost no time to accompany your child, but hope that your child will excel in studies and be outstanding, this is an unrealistic expectation in itself. A child's growth is not just the accumulation of knowledge, but also the inheritance of values, behaviors, and learning methods. If parents are absent for a long time, it is difficult for children to truly learn "how to be a person, how to do things, and how to learn".

The most irreplaceable thing in education is the investment of time.

Many people think that it is enough to leave the child to grandparents or nannies. But intergenerational parenting is often prone to spoiling, and children who lack parental companionship for a long time are prone to emotional imbalance, and even addicted to games or short videos. The core of the problem is often not the child himself, but the long-term absence of parents.

As the first responsible person for the child, if you have a child but outsource the main parenting responsibility for a long time, this is a question that needs reflection. The reason for many parents' absence is "to make money". In order to make money, they have to leave their children to others to take care of, but they expect their children to have excellent grades or even surpass themselves. This logic itself is contradictory.

More realistically, many people are busy all year round, but have not truly achieved financial freedom. Stopping will cause anxiety, so continue to rush. The pressure of social "involution" will also be reflected in individuals - complaining about fierce competition while having to participate in it. Some people work with high intensity for a long time, overdrawing their health, and even sudden death occurs.

Why is this state difficult to change? On the one hand, it is a problem of system and environment, and on the other hand, it is also the result of culture and group pressure. In collective culture, individuals have to pay a high price to fight against the mainstream rhythm. Even if they oppose involution verbally, they still have to work overtime, lay off employees, and cut salaries in reality. Ordinary people often find it difficult to influence the general environment.

But the fact that the general environment is difficult to change immediately does not mean that individuals have no choice.

You can choose not to pursue money excessively. You can choose to reserve time to accompany your child. You can choose to spend limited energy on truly important things.

What can truly change a child's destiny is not that you plan him to be "someone else's child", but whether you appear stably for a long time in his growth process.
