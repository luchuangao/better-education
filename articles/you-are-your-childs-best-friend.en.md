# You Are Your Child's Best Friend

Since I was a child, the only goal my family gave me was to study hard. Other than that, they gave me almost no important guidance in other aspects, and even made me feel that I didn't even have the right to ask questions. I gradually realized that "poor children take charge of their families early" is actually a misunderstanding. It wasn't until I was thirty years old that I truly understood the meaning of home. Many people think home is where they were born or the address on their household registration book, but to me, the real home is where the heart is at ease. Many parents also do not have clear concepts about many things, so most questions can only be explored and learned step by step by themselves.

Now that I have my own child, I no longer only require him to study well. I will seriously answer every question he has and tell him my views and opinions on things, not just the knowledge learned at school. No matter where the child goes in the future, he will know that mom and dad are his best friends, and we will always share our thoughts and ways of handling problems with him.

Recently, my son likes to play with several children a few years older than him. During holidays, most children of the same age stay at home, and only these older children can play together. Although they occasionally bully him and he can't beat them, he still wants to play with them. I told him: if he hits you again, I will warn him not to bully you. If he still bullies you, you can choose not to play with him, and dad will play with you, because dad is your best friend. In fact, when he meets those children, he still plays with them without holding a grudge. Children are like this, playing and fighting, and holding a grudge is only temporary. As long as it is not excessive, I allow him to handle such problems himself, as a parent there is no need to intervene excessively. But he knows that I am always his backbone, a friend who can reach out to help him at any time.

Since my son was very young, the relationship between me and him has been more like friends. He will ask me all kinds of questions, and I will answer them patiently. We also often play fighting games together, and I always pretend to lose to him. Traditional educational concepts often emphasize strict fathers and kind mothers, but I don't think there must be a strict father. In my home, mom is a little stricter, while I chose to be a gentler father role. Whenever there is a problem, he is willing to tell mom and dad and is happy to share his way of handling it. At present, our relationship is very harmonious. Even if he grows up in the future and his desire to share may decrease, he will always understand: parents are his best friends.
