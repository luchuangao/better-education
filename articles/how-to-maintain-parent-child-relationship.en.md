# How to Maintain Parent-Child Relationship

How to maintain the parent-child relationship? In the last article, I wrote that "my child and I are more like partners" rather than a father-son relationship. From the moment the child was born, I realized that he is an independent individual, a new member of the family, with his own thoughts and personality.

Today, I continue to share some personal educational insights as an unqualified father.

## First, I learned silence from a young age, but I let my child express freely.

When I was young, I liked to ask "why" like other children, but my parents often said to me "it's useless to think so much" or interrupted me with "have you finished your homework?". During festivals, I could only sit at the children's table and prick up my ears to listen to the adults chatting. Thinking about it now, it was nothing more than family gossip, children's grades, making money and becoming an official, and marriage talk, which is not essentially different from young people talking about celebrity gossip and workplace experiences today. I tried to ask a few sentences, but was always blocked back by "why worry about this?". Over time, I learned silence. Later, family and relatives always asked me: "What are you thinking about all day?" In fact, I just wasn't interested in their conversation.

Therefore, since my child could speak and ask "why", I have answered every question. There is no question that cannot be asked, and no answer that must be avoided. If I encounter something I don't know, I check it with him.

## Second, I only had labor from a young age, I let my child participate in housework.

I grew up in the countryside, and like most children, I had to work in the fields, wash clothes and cook. At that time, I rejected this day-after-day labor of "facing the loess and back to the sky", but it is undeniable that these experiences shaped me and made me understand the relationship between giving and gaining.

After moving to the city, living among reinforced concrete, housework became the most daily "labor". Sweeping, washing clothes, and washing dishes are all family matters. My wife even let the child pick up a kitchen knife to learn to cut vegetables when he was four years old, and he cut his hand because of it. Now he is six years old, and doing housework has gradually become a family obligation he identifies with, from initial curiosity and fun. I think every member should contribute to the family, which is what a healthy family should look like.

In addition, in the past two years, when we eat on weekends, we also hold a small family discussion meeting. Vote for the "head of the family" of the week. Everyone discusses what happened this week together. In my opinion, the equality of rights and obligations is the basis for a lasting and harmonious family relationship.

## Third, the rebellious me, and the rebellious him.

I have been a rebellious child since I was young, and I rarely satisfied my parents. After dropping out of high school, my father once said to me: "Your life is over." I still don't agree with this sentence.

My son is also often disobedient, and I even encourage him to make mistakes. In my opinion, a certain degree of resistance and trial and error is benign rebellion, and it is the only way to learn independent thinking and move towards growth. At home, he doesn't need to suppress his thoughts and emotions. I often see my childhood self in him. And what I can do is to have more tolerance and patience to let him know: home is always a harbor where he can dock, a haven for emotions.

This is my way of maintaining the parent-child relationship. As an unqualified father, I know there is still too much to learn.
