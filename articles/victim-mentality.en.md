# Victim Mentality

I have often had a three-minute passion for doing things since I was a child, and relatives around me often say so. Over time, I gradually formed a psychology of learned helplessness. Today I want to talk about this strong victim mentality in me, why it appeared, and why it still traps me today.

First of all, there were too many denials during growth. If I didn't do well, I would be criticized and rarely encouraged. This made me particularly sensitive to the outside world and hesitant in doing things. Even if I finally started, it often only lasted for a few days. Even if I clearly knew that some things were worth long-term investment, I was always attracted by short-term returns and soon felt powerless. Some people say: "Happy people heal their lives with their childhood, while unhappy people heal their childhood with their lives." I think I belong to the latter.

If I stay in the victim mentality, my life is like faded, dim and lightless. Therefore, I often escape from people and things, close myself off, sometimes starve for two or three days, and sometimes sleep for a whole day, until my body can't stand it anymore, and then slowly recover the rhythm. Such a cycle appears repeatedly. I had a test before, and I also have a tendency of severe depression, but I have never truly understood where the problem lies.

Recently I talked with AI, and it helped me point out a key point: I lived in fear and a sense of failure since I was a child. Such an environment has shaped my nervous system for a long time, making me over-alert to everything and always in a state of defense. AI also gave some suggestions: stop fighting with your past self, but reduce the long-term tension of nerves by standardizing life, such as regular sleep, low-intensity exercise, regulating breathing, rebuilding cognitive ways, and so on. Looking back, if I could have gotten rid of this mentality earlier, several important turning points in my life might not have been abandoned halfway because of powerlessness.

I admit I have a victim mentality. And the purpose of admission is my self-awareness to change, which is at the psychological level, different from the biological level. On the one hand, I hope to realize my life value; on the other hand, as a father, I realize that this mentality will be passed on to children. Only by healing oneself can one illuminate others. I am not a qualified father, but I want to try hard to encourage children more, criticize and deny less, and not hit or scold, to create a relaxed and pleasant growth environment for children, and think about problems from his perspective. To achieve these, any amount of self-reminders are worth it.

Why I always say I am not a qualified father is also a self-confirmation of the victim mentality. When one day I say I am a qualified father, it proves that I have indeed gotten rid of the victim mentality.
